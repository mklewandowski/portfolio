Hi, I'm Matt Lewandowski

I am a versatile, accomplished Senior Software Engineer and Game Designer. I thrive on making impactful software with creative individuals in adaptable teams. I'm particularly interested in the intersection of STEM education, gaming, and storytelling.

Projects

About Me I'm a software engineer and game designer located in the Bay Area. I spent a decade working in biotech and medical research software where I built cardiovascular imaging applications; led installations and trainings in the US, Canada, South Korea, and Japan; and collaborated with corporate partners across North America, Europe, and East Asia. After winning a videogame development competition, my brother and I founded Team2Bit, a game studio that has shipped titles on console, PC, and mobile and partnered with publishers like Adult Swim. I helped build a suite of award-winning mobile apps with over 2 million downloads at UC Berkeley. I worked on over a dozen science education projects at the Concord Consortium including digital simulations used in standardized tests in the state of Massachusetts. I'm currently working on a NASA-funded AR solar system walk, building radio education applications, and creating a voxel adventure game. When time permits, I also create small, quirky games that I release on itch.io.

a little more...

When I'm not building software, I like to spend my time traveling, hiking, biking, swimming, practicing yoga, weightlifting, studying film and videogame history, and keeping tabs on the neighborhood crows. I also enjoy mentoring folks interested in game and software development and have worked with Girls Make Games, Gameheads, and UC San Diego's Neurodiversity in Tech internship program to help prepare the next generation of creators.

DIY Solar System

DIY Solar System is a mobile application that explores our solar system through activities and interactives including an AR planet walk. DIY Solar System is a collaboration between the Children's Creativity Museum in San Francisco, UC Berkeley, and the Sciencenter of Ithaca, NY. It will be released in 2023.

Tech Stack Highlights: C# and Unity

Role: Interactive designer and lead developer

Snow My Gosh

Snow My Gosh is an over-the-shoulder, voxel art, infinite driving game. Snow My Gosh is built for use in browsers and on mobile devices and can be played on itch.io.

Tech Stack Highlights: C#, Unity, MagicaVoxel

Role: Artist, designer, engineer

Future Thinker Poll

The Future Thinker Poll is a mobile application used in the "Future Visions 2071" exhibit at Arizona State University's Center for Science and the Imagination. The exhibit is a NASA collaboration which envisions possible future timelines based on scientific research.
